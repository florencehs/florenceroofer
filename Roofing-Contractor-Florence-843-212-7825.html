At particular times of the year, Florence, SC can experience strong winds and storms, therefore you will require a more long lasting and stable roofing protection system. Despite having the highest quality of roofing and the best roofing system style, you still end up having repairs-- and often even changing your roofing-- after a major storm like we experienced with Hurricanes Matthew, Charlie, and Hugo.

To help minimize, if not totally remove, repairs or roofing system problems you will need to deal with after storms and strong winds, we have gathered these roofing protection concepts from industry experts and experts:

Practical Roofing Security Concepts

  • Deal with an extremely knowledgeable designer or roof expert.

    A Florence roofing expert will assist you develop a long lasting roofing system for your house, depending upon your place, the style of your house and in what wind direction your house is dealing with. Working with licensed and licensed designers will likewise help you ensure that your roofing follows the requirements of your insurance coverage, local codes and standards, and resistant to wind uplift and fires.


  • Pay more attention to your roof's edges and corners.

    Although they may seem more for ornamental purposes, the corners and edges of your roofing system really assist keep the entire roof in place even when there is a strong wind. To make sure that your roofing system's edges and corners will not fail you throughout a windstorm, ensure they have constant cleats, gravel stops, and copings.


  • Ensure mechanical units are tightly protected.

    These mechanical systems mostly include your air conditioner and their screen covers, antennae, and satellite dishes. Most often, damages to roofing come from the mechanical systems attached to it. If these are too loose, they may pull your roofing and cause damage to it. Make sure all units and gadgets that you set up to your house through your roofing are firmly secured. Use long screws if essential.


  • Examine your roofing system for vulnerable points.

    Before the storm season or throughout a break in the strong wind environment, have a professional examine your roofing system for weak spots. These may be torn flashings, loose boundary accessories, and weak gravel stops and copings. Should you find any, carry out the needed steps to secure these vulnerable points. If you must, strengthen gravel stops and copings to be sure that you secure them more firmly and that they won't quickly fall off.


  • Devote to regular cleansing.

    Set a regular schedule of cleaning your roofing system. It is exceptionally crucial that you keep your roofing free from particles like dried leaves and fallen branches. If these build up on your roofing system, especially on the drain, you will soon have leakage and mold problems even if there were no strong winds and/or storms.


  • Conduct routine roof study.

    It is important that you keep a journal of the existing condition of your roof prior to, after and during storms. This will in fact help your roofing expert execute the required and most suitable fixes and repairs to your roofing. Plus, it will also make it much easier for your insurance provider to authorize your claims. Ensure your roofer, expert, or architect indications every entry you make in the journal.


Your roofing system safeguards your house and your household, so it is just fitting that you secure it, too. Help it go through windstorms totally untouched by working just with certified roofing specialists.
